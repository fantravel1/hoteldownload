Any prices, price comparisons, or savings estimates mentioned on HotelDownload.com are point-in-time observations and may not reflect current rates. Always verify prices directly on the booking platform at the time of your reservation.

Standards

Evidence Policy

How we evaluate and classify the strength of evidence behind each strategy and recommendation.

Tier A

Strong Evidence

Strategies and claims supported by multiple independent sources, large-scale testing across markets, or peer-reviewed research. Tier A evidence has been verified repeatedly and is widely accepted within the travel industry. Examples include: comparing prices across multiple platforms yields lower prices, and booking directly with hotels can provide price-match benefits.

Tier B

Moderate Evidence

Strategies supported by our own controlled testing, reputable third-party reports, or consistent user reports. Tier B evidence is reliable but may not have been verified across all markets or conditions. We have high confidence in these strategies but acknowledge some variability in results. Examples include: mobile app prices being lower than desktop, and certain days of the week offering better rates.

Tier C

Limited Evidence

Strategies based on smaller-scale testing, individual case studies, or emerging patterns that require further verification. Tier C evidence suggests a strategy may work but has not been sufficiently validated across diverse scenarios. We include these strategies for completeness but recommend users test them independently. Examples include: certain VPN locations yielding different prices, and seasonal booking windows for specific regions.

Tier D

Anecdotal / Unverified

Claims based on anecdotal reports, unverified user submissions, or widely repeated travel tips that lack controlled testing. Tier D items are included with clear labeling so users are aware of the limited supporting evidence. We do not recommend relying on Tier D strategies without independent verification. These are included to document commonly discussed tactics and to flag claims that may be myths.
