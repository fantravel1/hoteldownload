FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about hotel booking websites answered.

The best hotel booking website depends on your needs. Booking.com offers the widest selection with 28M+ listings. Google Hotels is best for price comparison across multiple sites. Expedia excels at bundle deals (hotel + flight). For luxury and boutique hotels, consider Mr & Mrs Smith or Tablet Hotels. For last-minute deals, HotelTonight offers the best discounts.

OTAs (Online Travel Agencies) like Booking.com and Expedia are booking platforms where you complete your reservation directly. Meta-search engines like Google Hotels, Trivago, and Kayak compare prices across multiple OTAs and redirect you to book on the chosen platform. Meta-search helps find the lowest price, while OTAs handle the actual booking.

Mobile apps often offer exclusive discounts of 10-20% compared to websites. Booking.com, Expedia, Hotels.com, and Agoda all promote mobile-only rates. However, websites may be easier for comparing multiple options with multiple tabs open. For the best price, check both the mobile app and website before booking.

Booking directly with hotels often provides: best rate guarantees, free room upgrades, loyalty points, flexible cancellation, and better customer service. However, third-party sites may offer lower upfront prices, bundle deals, or reward programs. Compare both options for each booking to maximize value.

Wholesale hotel websites like Costco Travel and AAA Travel offer pre-negotiated rates through membership programs. These sites often include extras like gift cards, resort credits, or breakfast. Rates are typically non-commissionable, meaning hotels provide genuine discounts rather than just waiving OTA commissions.

Flash sale sites like Secret Escapes and Jetsetter offer limited-time deals on luxury hotels at 30-60% off. These sales typically last 1-7 days and require membership (usually free). Hotels participate to fill rooms during low-demand periods while maintaining rate parity on public channels.

HotelTonight is the leading last-minute booking website, specializing in same-day and next-day reservations with discounts up to 50%. Other options include Booking.com's "Tonight Only" deals, Priceline Express Deals, and Hotwire Hot Rates. Last-minute deals are best for flexible travelers who can accept mystery hotels or limited options.

For OTAs: Hotels.com offers a free night after 10 stays, Booking.com Genius provides 10-20% discounts at higher levels, and Expedia One Key combines rewards across Expedia, Hotels.com, and Vrbo. For direct booking: Marriott Bonvoy, Hilton Honors, IHG One Rewards, and World of Hyatt offer points, elite status, and exclusive benefits.

For Europe: Booking.com dominates with extensive coverage. For Asia: Agoda, Trip.com (Ctrip), and Klook offer local expertise and competitive rates. For India: MakeMyTrip and Goibibo are market leaders. For global coverage, Google Hotels helps compare regional and international platforms simultaneously.

Hotel comparison websites like Google Hotels, Trivago, and Kayak are generally accurate but may not include all booking sources. Some hotels have exclusive partnerships or rate parity agreements that affect displayed prices. Always verify the final price on the booking site, as taxes, fees, and currency conversion may differ from the comparison display.

Look for: resort fees (common in Las Vegas and Hawaii), destination fees, parking charges, early check-in/late check-out fees, and taxes not included in the displayed rate. Booking.com typically shows total prices including taxes. Use the filter for "all-inclusive pricing" when available, and always review the full breakdown before confirming.

Booking.com is known for extensive free cancellation options (look for the green "Free Cancellation" badge). Expedia, Hotels.com, and most major OTAs offer refundable rates, though they may cost 10-20% more than non-refundable options. Direct hotel bookings often provide the most flexible cancellation policies, especially for loyalty members.
